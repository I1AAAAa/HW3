Summary of All Functions

Below is a simple reference list of all eight Jungian functions:

  • Ni — Introverted Intuition
  • Ne — Extraverted Intuition
  • Ti — Introverted Thinking
  • Te — Extraverted Thinking
  • Fi — Introverted Feeling
  • Fe — Extraverted Feeling
  • Si — Introverted Sensing
  • Se — Extraverted Sensing

Understanding the 8 Cognitive Functions

Each Jungian cognitive function represents a unique way of perceiving or judging the world. Below are simplified explanations with example behaviors.

The 16 Personality Types

Below are descriptions of all 16 personality types based on Carl Jung's cognitive function theory.

ESTJ — The Organizer

Dominant Function: Extraverted Thinking (Te)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Sensing (Si)

  • Organize — Creates structure and order in their environment.
  • Lead — Natural leadership abilities and decisive action.
  • Create order — Establishes systems and procedures.
  • Enforce rules — Values tradition and established standards.
  • Achieve goals — Focused on practical results and efficiency.

ISTJ — The Traditionalist

Dominant Function: Introverted Sensing (Si)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Thinking (Te)

  • Uphold tradition — Values established customs and practices.
  • Ensure stability — Prefers consistent and predictable environments.
  • Maintain order — Organized and methodical approach to life.
  • Provide reliability — Dependable and responsible in commitments.

ESTP — The Adventurer

Dominant Function: Extraverted Sensing (Se)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Thinking (Ti)

  • Act spontaneously — Responds quickly to immediate situations.
  • Solve problems — Pragmatic and hands-on problem solving.
  • Enjoy the moment — Lives in the present with enthusiasm.
  • Adapt — Flexible and responsive to changing circumstances.

ISTP — The Craftsman

Dominant Function: Introverted Thinking (Ti)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Sensing (Se)

  • Solve problems — Analytical and logical problem-solving approach.
  • Adapt quickly — Flexible and resourceful in unexpected situations.
  • Master practical tasks — Skilled with tools and hands-on work.
  • Explore — Independent and curious about how things work.

ESFJ — The Caregiver

Dominant Function: Extraverted Feeling (Fe)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Sensing (Si)

  • Foster harmony — Promotes cooperation and positive relationships.
  • Support others — Nurturing and attentive to others' needs.
  • Organize — Creates structured and welcoming environments.
  • Maintain social connections — Values community and interpersonal bonds.

ISFJ — The Protector

Dominant Function: Introverted Sensing (Si)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Feeling (Fe)

  • Nurture others — Warm and caring toward people they care about.
  • Preserve harmony — Avoids conflict and seeks peaceful resolution.
  • Create comfort — Makes others feel safe and supported.
  • Support quietly — Humble and modest in helping others.

ESFP — The Entertainer

Dominant Function: Extraverted Sensing (Se)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Feeling (Fi)

  • Spread enthusiasm — Brings energy and excitement to social situations.
  • Enjoy life — Lives in the moment with zest and spontaneity.
  • Create experiences — Makes life fun and memorable for others.
  • Entertain others — Natural ability to engage and delight people.

ISFP — The Artist

Dominant Function: Introverted Feeling (Fi)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Sensing (Se)

  • Create harmony — Values peace and avoids conflict.
  • Express self — Artistic and creative in personal expression.
  • Appreciate beauty — Sensitive to aesthetics and sensory experiences.
  • Explore values — Independent and authentic in personal beliefs.

ENTJ — The Commander

Dominant Function: Extraverted Thinking (Te)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Intuition (Ni)

  • Lead — Natural leader with strong organizational skills.
  • Organize — Creates efficient systems and structures.
  • Strategize — Long-term planning and vision.
  • Achieve goals — Driven and goal-oriented.
  • Command efficiently — Takes charge and directs effectively.

INTJ — The Strategic Thinker

Dominant Function: Introverted Intuition (Ni)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Thinking (Te)

  • Strategize — Sees long-term patterns and future possibilities.
  • Innovate — Creative problem-solving with novel approaches.
  • Achieve efficiency — Focuses on optimal solutions.
  • Pursue intellectual growth — Constantly learning and improving.

ENFJ — The Teacher

Dominant Function: Extraverted Feeling (Fe)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Intuition (Ni)

  • Motivate — Inspires others to reach their potential.
  • Inspire — Charismatic and visionary in leadership.
  • Empathize — Understands and responds to others' emotions.
  • Lead — Natural ability to guide and influence.
  • Help others grow — Dedicated to personal development of others.

INFJ — The Advocate

Dominant Function: Introverted Intuition (Ni)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Feeling (Fe)

  • Inspire change — Visionary with a desire to make a positive impact.
  • Advocate ideals — Stands up for values and causes they believe in.
  • Understand others — Deep insight into people's motivations.
  • Create vision — Sees possibilities and future potential.

ENTP — The Debater

Dominant Function: Extraverted Intuition (Ne)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Thinking (Ti)

  • Debate — Enjoys intellectual discussions and challenges.
  • Innovate — Creative and original in thinking.
  • Explore ideas — Fascinated by possibilities and concepts.
  • Challenge norms — Questions assumptions and conventional thinking.
  • Adapt — Flexible and quick to adjust strategies.

INTP — The Thinker

Dominant Function: Introverted Thinking (Ti)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Intuition (Ne)

  • Analyze — Logical and systematic in understanding systems.
  • Innovate — Creative problem-solving with theoretical frameworks.
  • Seek knowledge — Driven by curiosity and understanding.
  • Understand complexity — Comfortable with abstract and intricate ideas.

ENFP — The Inspired Creator

Dominant Function: Extraverted Intuition (Ne)
Auxiliary Function: Introverted Feeling (Fi)

  • Inspire change — Enthusiastic about possibilities and improvement.
  • Generate ideas — Creative and abundant in concepts.
  • Explore possibilities — Open-minded and curious about the world.
  • Motivate others — Energetic and encouraging to those around them.

INFP — The Mediator

Dominant Function: Introverted Feeling (Fi)
Auxiliary Function: Extraverted Intuition (Ne)

  • Seek meaning — Values authenticity and personal significance.
  • Express individuality — Creative and unique in self-expression.
  • Advocate ideals — Passionate about values and causes.
  • Inspire others — Encouraging and supportive of others' growth.
